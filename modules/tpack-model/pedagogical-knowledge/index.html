Pedagogical Knowledge (PK)

Pedagogical Knowledge (PK) refers to knowledge of teaching methods and strategies, different learning theories, evaluation methods, classroom management, and ways of engaging students in learning activities.

Teaching strategies:
  • A teacher uses strategies such as problem-based learning, guided discovery, cooperative learning, or questioning techniques to help students actively construct mathematical understanding.
Classroom management:
  • Organizing group work during a mathematics lesson, setting clear rules for discussion, and managing time effectively reflect pedagogical knowledge.
Evaluation and feedback:
  • A teacher uses formative assessment techniques such as quizzes, exit tickets, or oral questioning to check students' understanding and provide immediate feedback.
