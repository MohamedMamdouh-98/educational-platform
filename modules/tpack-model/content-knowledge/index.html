Content Knowledge (CK)

Content Knowledge (CK) refers to the knowledge of the subject matter to be taught, including its core concepts, principles, facts, procedures, and the relationships among them.

When solving a linear equation, the teacher not only applies the steps correctly but also explains why each step is valid, such as why the same operation must be applied to both sides of the equation.

A teacher demonstrates CK by showing the relationship between fractions, decimals, and percentages, and explaining how these concepts represent the same value in different forms.

Examples:

Finding the limit of a function at a given value.

Deriving the formula for the area of a triangle.

Explaining the concept of simple probability.

Explaining the relationship between the angle and the circumference of a circle.
